Vikram Sarabhai

1919 - 1979

Vikram Sarabhai was a visionary scientist who played a pivotal role in establishing India's space program.

Vikram Sarabhai was a visionary scientist and innovator who played a crucial role in shaping India's modern identity. As the founder of India's space program, he pioneered the use of space technology for national development, paving the way for India to become a major player in the global space industry. His relentless pursuit of excellence and dedication to his work continue to inspire generations of scientists, entrepreneurs, and leaders in India and beyond.

The Legacy of Vikram Sarabhai

Vikram Sarabhai's legacy lives on today in the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), which he founded in 1969. His contributions to the field of space research were immense, and he played a key role in shaping India's space program. He was a firm believer in using technology for the betterment of society, and his work in developing India's first satellite, Aryabhata, paved the way for many more successes to come. Today, ISRO is one of the world's leading space agencies, and continues to inspire generations of scientists and engineers to push the boundaries of space exploration. Vikram Sarabhai's vision and passion for science and technology have left an indelible mark on India's scientific landscape, and his legacy continues to inspire and influence countless individuals around the world.

Timeline

1919 Vikram Sarabhai was born on August 12th in Ahmedabad, India.
1942 Completed his undergraduate degree in Physics from St. John's College, University of Cambridge.
1945 Completed his Ph.D. in Cosmic Ray Physics from the University of Cambridge.
1947 Returned to India and established the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL) in Ahmedabad.
1957 Founded the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR), which later became the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO).
1962 Launched India's first sounding rocket, which was developed under Vikram Sarabhai's leadership.
1972 Became the President of the General Conference of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA).
1973 Received the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian awards.
1975 Served as the Chairman of the Atomic Energy Commission of India.
1979 Vikram Sarabhai passed away on December 30th at the age of 59.
